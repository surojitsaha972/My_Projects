Neurodegenerative Diseases

Adrenoleukodystrophy (ALD)

ALD is a genetic disorder that primarily affects the white matter of the brain and the adrenal glands. It results from the accumulation of very long-chain fatty acids (VLCFAs) due to a defective peroxisomal enzyme. This leads to demyelination, the destruction of the protective myelin sheath surrounding nerve fibers.

Lorenzo's oil (a mixture of erucic acid and oleic acid) can slow the progression of some forms of ALD. Hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT) and gene therapy are showing promising results in clinical trials.
  • Blood tests to measure VLCFA levels.
  • MRI of the brain to assess white matter abnormalities.
  • Genetic testing to identify mutations in the ABCD1 gene.
  • HSCT can halt the progression of cerebral ALD if performed early.
  • Lorenzo's oil can slow the progression of some forms.
  • Gene therapy is an emerging treatment option.
  • Adrenal insufficiency treatment.
 Significant progress in gene therapy, offering potential for long-term disease modification.

Alzheimer's Disease (AD)

It is a progressive brain disorder that causes memory loss, cognitive decline, and behavioral changes. It is the most common cause of dementia, leading to difficulties in daily life. It results from abnormal protein buildup in the brain, damaging cells over time.

Alexander Disease

A rare progressive leukodystrophy characterized by the abnormal accumulation of glial fibrillary acidic protein (GFAP) in astrocytes, the brain's support cells.

Drug Names Newest Drugs Diagnosis Treatment Recent Advances
There are no specific medications to cure or reverse Alexander disease. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms. Research into GFAP targeted therapies is ongoing.
  • MRI of the brain to identify characteristic white matter abnormalities.
  • Genetic testing to confirm mutations in the GFAP gene.
  • Symptomatic management, including physical therapy, occupational therapy, and medications to control seizures.
  • Supportive care.
  • Increased understanding of the role of GFAP in the disease process.
  • Research into potential gene therapies.
