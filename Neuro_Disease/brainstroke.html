Neurodegenerative Diseases

Brain Stroke

A brain stroke is the loss of blood flow to the brain, causing tissue damage due to oxygen deprivation.

Drug Names Newest Drugs Diagnosis Treatment Recent Advances
  • Thrombolytics: Alteplase (tPA)
  • Antiplatelets: Aspirin, Clopidogrel
  • Anticoagulants: Warfarin, Dabigatran, Rivaroxaban
  • Anticonvulsants: Diazepam
  • Antihypertensives: Labetalol, Nicardipine
  • Osmotic Agents: Mannitol
  • Statins: Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin
  • Others: ACE inhibitors, ARBs
  • Tenecteplase
  • Clinical Assessment: Check symptoms (e.g., weakness, slurred speech) and perform neurological exams.
  • Imaging:CT scan, MRI, Angiography
  • Blood Tests: Assess clotting, glucose, and cholesterol levels.
  • ECG: Detects heart conditions like atrial fibrillation.
  • Carotid Ultrasound: Evaluates blood flow in carotid arteries.
  • Cerebral Angiography: Confirms vascular issues when needed.
  • Ischemic Stroke (Clot):
    Thrombolytics (e.g., tPA): Clot-busting drugs within 3-4.5 hours.
    Thrombectomy: Clot removal within 6-24 hours.
  • Haemorrhagic Stroke (Bleed):
    Control bleeding: Medications and surgery (e.g., craniotomy).
    Reverse anticoagulants if necessary.
  • Rehabilitation: Physical, speech, and occupational therapy.
  • Prevention: Blood pressure control, cholesterol management, and lifestyle changes.
  • MSUs: Specialized ambulances improve treatment speed by diagnosing and treating stroke in the field, reducing delays and improving outcomes.
  • Haemorrhage Protocols: New blood pressure management saves lives.
  • Thrombectomy: Enhanced clot removal improves recovery.
