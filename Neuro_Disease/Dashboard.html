Neurodegenerative Diseases

Neurodegenerative diseases are a class of progressive disorders that disrupt the brain and nervous system, leading to a steady decline in cognitive, motor, and functional abilities. Driven by factors like protein misfolding, oxidative stress, and neuroinflammation, these conditions gradually erode neural function. Though currently incurable, treatments focus on slowing progression and enhancing quality of life.
