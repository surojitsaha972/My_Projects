Neurodegenerative Diseases

Alexander Disease

A rare progressive leukodystrophy characterized by the abnormal accumulation of glial fibrillary acidic protein (GFAP) in astrocytes, the brain's support cells.

Drug Names Newest Drugs Diagnosis Treatment Recent Advances
There are no specific medications to cure or reverse Alexander disease. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms. Research into GFAP targeted therapies is ongoing.
  • MRI of the brain to identify characteristic white matter abnormalities.
  • Genetic testing to confirm mutations in the GFAP gene.
  • Symptomatic management, including physical therapy, occupational therapy, and medications to control seizures.
  • Supportive care.
  • Increased understanding of the role of GFAP in the disease process.
  • Research into potential gene therapies.
