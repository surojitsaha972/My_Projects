Neurodegenerative Diseases

Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)

It is a progressive disease-causing muscle weakness and paralysis by damaging motor neurons in the brain and spinal cord.

  • Clinical Exam: Identifies progressive weakness and motor dysfunction.
  • EMG & NCS: Confirms motor neuron damage, excludes nerve disorders.
  • MRI: Rules out structural brain or spine issues.
  • Blood/Genetic Tests: Screens for mimicking diseases and ALS-linked mutations.
  • El Escorial Criteria: Validates ALS by motor neuron involvement in multiple regions.
  • medications: Riluzole (Slows progression)-Edaravone (Reduces oxidative stress)
  • Symptom Management: Muscle relaxants, antidepressants, and pain relief.
  • therapies: Physical, occupational, speech therapy.
  • nutrition: High-calorie diet, feeding tubes.
  • Respiratory Support: BiPAP, mechanical ventilation.
  • Multidisciplinary Care: Neurologists, therapists, dietitians, palliative care.
  • biomarkers: Predict drug response for personalized care.
  • neuroimaging: Reveals CNS changes in ALS.
  • Stem Cell Therapy: Spinal cord injections show potential in slowing disease progression.
  • Assistive Tech: Tools enhance daily living.
  • Synapse Regeneration: SPG302, a pill targeting synapse repair, is in phase 2 trial.
